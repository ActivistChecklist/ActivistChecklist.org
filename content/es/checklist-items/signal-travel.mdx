[Signal](https://signal.org/) is an important app that we recommend using to keep your communications secure.

### Securing Signal at the border

<HowTo title="How to secure signal for a border crossing">
  1. **Delete Signal threads** with sensitive communication
  2. **Leave Signal groups** that you're in with people you don't want to be easily associated with. (Make a list of these groups and ask an admin in the group to re-add you after you cross the border.)
  3. **Hide the app:** You can move an app to a hidden folder. Instructions: [iPhone](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/lock-or-hide-or-an-app-iph00f208d05/ios) and [Android](https://support.google.com/android/answer/15341885?hl=en). This will only provide a slight advantage, because most attackers know to look in this folder.
</HowTo>

**Tip:** If you've kept [disappearing messages](/signal/) turned on for all your threads, you might not have much deleting to do!

### If you're willing to uninstall Signal

While uninstalling Signal is the most secure option, it can be very disruptive.

**Because it is such an inconvenience, we only recommend uninstalling Signal if you have a lot of high-risk messages.** If you do, you might consider following the "High Security Approach" section instead.

**Benefits of uninstalling Signal:**

* Your communications are more protected from a basic search

**Downsides to uninstalling Signal:**

* Your entire message history is deleted
* Your groups are not findable until someone messages you again in that group

If you're bringing your laptop, consider uninstalling the Signal desktop app as well.
