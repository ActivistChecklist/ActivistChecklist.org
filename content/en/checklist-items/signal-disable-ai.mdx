Google and Apple haven't made it clear when their AI tools are sending our private information (like Signal messages) off of our device onto their services. If they are sent to their servers, they are at risk of being accessed via a government court order.

Our understanding is that these AI features ***currently*** only summarize notifications. But with the way things are going, they might try to be more invasive.

Read our article on [Is Apple Intelligence reading my Signal messages?](/signal-ai/)

<HowTo title="How to protect your Signal messages.">
  **OPTION A: Disable AI notifications summaries just for Signal**

  * *On iPhone:* Open the Settings app > Notifications > Summarize Notifications > **Toggle off Signal**
  * *On Android:* Settings > Notifications > Notification summaries > Manage apps (under "Don't include these apps") > **Toggle off Signal**

  **OPTION B: Entirely Disable AI notification summaries**

  * *On your iPhone:* Open the Settings app on your iPhone > Apple Intelligence & Siri > **Toggle off "Apple Intelligence"**
  * *On Android:* Open Settings > Notifications > **Toggle off "Use notification summaries"** or "Notification summaries"
  * *On your Mac:* If you have an Apple computer with an M1 processor, you can disable Apple Intelligence by going to  > System Settings > Apple Intelligence & Siri > **Toggle off "Apple Intelligence"** > Confirm
</HowTo>
