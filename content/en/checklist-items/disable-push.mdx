Push notifications are notifications that are sent through Apple/Google's server to be able to notify you about something that happened inside the app.

For example: On a messaging app, a push notification could include sensitive information such as the name of the person who sent you a message and a preview of the message content.

You can protect yourself by either disabling notifications from sensitive apps entirely or by hiding the name/preview on the notification.

In 2023, [we learned](https://www.wired.com/story/apple-google-push-notification-surveillance/) that Apple and Google can be [compelled by a court order](https://www.reuters.com/technology/apple-now-requires-judges-consent-hand-over-push-notification-data-2023-12-12/) to turn over a list of a user's push notifications. For context, Apple turns over a [few hundred](https://www.apple.com/legal/transparency/push-token.html?ref=404media.co) push notifications each year, but they [receive thousands of requests](https://archive.is/20250604171935/https://www.404media.co/apple-gave-governments-data-on-thousands-of-push-notifications/).

<HowTo title="How to disable push notifications">

**Signal: **The most important app to hide push notifications for is Signal, because you may be using it for sensitive conversations.  Signal > Profile Picture > Settings > Notifications > Notification Content > **Select** "**No Name or Content**". This will leave notifications on but remove the name of the sender and the message preview.



**On iPhone:** Settings > Notifications > [App Name] > Show Previews > Select "Never"



**On Android:** Settings > Apps > [App Name] > Notifications > [Notification Category] > **Turn off "Show sensitive content on lock screen**" (or Settings > Notifications > [App Name] > Lock screen > Select "Hide sensitive content")

Some apps have settings within the app to just hide message previews and the sender's name instead of disabling the notification entirely.

Apps you may want to disable/hide notifications for:

- Messagings apps: Normal texts, Signal, WhatsApp, Slack, Facebook Messenger, Telegram, etc.
- Email: Gmail, Apple Mail, Proton Mail, etc.
- Social media DMs: Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn, dating apps, etc.

</HowTo>
