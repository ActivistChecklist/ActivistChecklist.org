When you give out your phone number to a group of unvetted strangers, they can easily find your real identity using [people search sites](/doxxing). 

You can protect yourself by only giving out your **[Signal username](https://signal.org/blog/phone-number-privacy-usernames/)**.

<HowTo title="How to set up your Signal username">

1. **Create a username:** Signal > Settings > Tap on your name/icon near the top > Tap the @ symbol > Edit username > **Set your username to something completely unrelated to your identity**, or any usernames you've used anywhere before. It must end in at least 2 numbers. (Tool: [Random username generator](https://strongphrase.net/username)).

   Example: `@CarefreeMoose.22`

2. **When you need people to contact you on Signal, tell them your Signal username** instead of your phone number. Usernames have two numbers at the end. If you give someone just your *display name*, they won't be able to find you.


*Note:* You can change your username as often as you want if you gave it out too widely and want only new folks to be able to find you. This won't effect the connections you've already made.

</HowTo>
