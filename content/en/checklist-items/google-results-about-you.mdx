Google offers [Results About You](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/12719076?hl=en) as a tool to help you know when your private information is shared online. It's not the most helpful in removing those results, but it will help you know when you need to manually find ways to get the information removed.

<HowTo title="How to enable Google Results About You">

1. **[Enable Google "Results About You"](https://myactivity.google.com/results-about-you)**: Fill in your full name (and any other names you use), addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses.
<Alert type="warning">

**Warning:** Do NOT enter **alias names** or **burner phone numbers** that you use exclusively for activism that are already fully separated from your true identity. You wouldn't want those linked up inside Google's systems.

</Alert>
2. **Wait** for the initial scan to complete (a few hours)
3. **Review the results and find ways to get them taken down:** 
1. Using the "request removal" button inside Google's tool is usually not helpful for two reasons: 1) Google will deny most requests unless they are from a "people search" site or intentional doxxing. And 2) the original information is still out there even if Google does remove it. People can find it on other search engines or the original site directly.
2. Instead, you want to try to contact the site owner to get the information removed. On websites, this means finding a contact form/email and telling them the page that has information you're asking to have removed. On social media sites, this might mean removing a post you made or asking the author to remove it.
3. Click "mark as reviewed" as you handle each item.
4. **Submit a Google "removal request":** Use [this removal request form](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/9673730?hl=en) to ask Google to remove search results that have your personal information or other doxxing content. This is different than Google's "Results About Me" feature.
5. **Watch for notification emails** about new results as they come up. You'll usually see the most in the first couple weeks after you sign up.

</HowTo>

*Privacy: *Requires a Google account. As with all of these opt-out services, there is a privacy trade-off. You're telling the company more information about you. This risk is usually worth it because they often have this information already, so there is little cost.
