If you have a strong password, disable face/fingerprint unlock, and [enable disk encryption](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/02/fbi-stymied-by-apples-lockdown-mode-after-seizing-journalists-iphone/), your laptop and other devices will be very hard to access. **Encryption** is an essential part of these defenses. Without it, your password and other steps are basically meaningless if the state seizes your device.

<HowTo title="How to enable encryption on your laptop (and backups)">

Encryption may need to be enabled on your laptop:

- **macOS**: System Settings > Privacy & Security [scroll to bottom] > FileVault > Click **Turn on... **> Save the **Recovery Key** in your password manager or somewhere secure > For maximum security, do not enable recovery using iCloud (but you will be required to remember your password and never lose it!)
- **Windows**: Settings > Privacy & Security > Device encryption > **Enable**


Also, make sure you have encryption turned of for your **external harddrives** and any backups you create.

</HowTo>

<Alert type="default">

**Phone encryption:** iPhones and modern Android phones are already encrypted by default.

</Alert>
