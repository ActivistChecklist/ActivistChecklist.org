Some of these sites make it very difficult to remove a profile, so services like EasyOptOuts can't do it for you. These usually require you to receive an email to confirm you are the person removing the profile.

<HowTo title="How to manually remove yourself from high-risk people search sites">

Start by manually opting out of the most high-risk sites:

1. **Opt-out of****[People Connect (Intelius)](https://suppression.peopleconnect.us/login)** - They own many people search sites, so one opt-out goes a long way. Fill out the "suppression form." You will be asked to verify your email/phone.
- If the site asks you to verify a phone number or email that you don’t have access to, call their technical support (877) 564-3253 and request to have your "personal information suppressed." Calling is much more effective than emailing them, in our experience.
2. **Search for yourself on****[National Public Data](https://nationalpublicdata.com/)****. **If you find a result, scroll to the bottom of the page and click Do Not Sell My Info to [opt-out](https://nationalpublicdata.com/optout.html).


There are other sites you'll only find by searching for your info manually on Google (explained in a [later checklist item](#manual-search)).

</HowTo>

**Fully manual: **If you would like to do all of the opt-outs manually, you can use Yael Grauer's [Big Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List](https://github.com/yaelwrites/Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List). Services like EasyOptOuts handle most of these for you, but not all of them. You could use this list to manually do the process at no cost, or manually search each database to see what you've missed.
