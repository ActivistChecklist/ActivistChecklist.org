---
title: Complete a comprehensive home safety walkthrough and take notes
---

Walk through your home and surrounding area to identify security strengths and vulnerabilities. Take notes on what you observe - you'll use these notes to prioritize improvements in the next section. Consider doing this with a trusted household member or friend for a second perspective. If you're a renter, focus on changes you can make without landlord approval and note any building-level security issues you might want to discuss with your landlord or advocate for with other tenants.

<HowTo title="How to do a home safety walkthrough">

1. Start a place to take notes
2. Walk around exterior of home and note entry points (doors, windows, balconies)
3. Test security of doors and windows - check locks and assess how easily they could be forced
4. Evaluate lighting around home exterior, hallways, and entrances
5. Note items outside that could help intruders (ladders, furniture, tools)
6. Assess building-level security (call boxes, multiple doors, building culture)
7. Check what's visible through windows from outside
8. Document current security measures already in place (locks, gates, fences, cameras, etc)

</HowTo>
