You've probably heard "turn off fingerprint/face unlock so cops can't [force](https://theintercept.com/2026/01/30/washington-post-hannah-natanson-fbi-biometrics-unlock-phone/) you to unlock your phone." Good advice—but it creates a problem. Without biometrics, people often set weak passcodes because they're entering them constantly. A weak passcode can be [cracked](https://sls.eff.org/technologies/forensic-extraction-tools).

#### **Our recommendation for lower-risk activists: Keep biometrics on, but know how to quick-disable them in high-risk situation. And practice often.**

This works if you'll generally have a moment to see risk coming (heading to a protest, crossing a border, etc.) and you trust yourself to remember.

<HowTo title="How to temporarily disable biometrics">

*On iPhone:* Tap the lock button (on the side) **5 times.**

*On Android:* Press and hold the power button. Press **"Lockdown"** *(If you don't see the "Lockdown" option, look for "Screen lock" settings to enable it.)*



<Alert type="warning">

Biometrics re-enable after you next enter your passcode. So remember to repeat this process as-needed.

</Alert>

</HowTo>

#### If your risk is less predictable—you're a known organizer, face possible search warrants, or know you'll forget to quick-disable in a crisis—fully disable biometrics in your settings instead.

<HowTo title="How to fully disable biometrics">

*On iPhone:* Settings > Face ID & Passcode > **Disable "iPhone Unlock"**

*On Android:* Settings > Security > Biometrics > **Disable both face/fingerprint for unlock**

</HowTo>
