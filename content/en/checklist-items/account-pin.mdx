---
title: Add a security PIN to your bank and cell phone accounts
slug: account-pin
---

Your bank accounts and cell phone provider account are high-value targets where you want to add extra protections. Some of these places will only try to verify with your phone number, address, and/or date of birth. All of these are kinds of information someone could find online about you. 

Adding a PIN or other security measures helps make sure you are the only person who has access.

<HowTo title="How to add a PIN to sensitive accounts">

**Here are some of the accounts you'll want to secure.**

1. Bank, credit card, loan, mortgage, and investment accounts
2. Your cell service provider (Verizon, T-Mobile, AT&T, etc.)
3. Internet provider
4. Utility companies
5. Insurance (health, home, auto, etc.)


**How to add a PIN for each account:**

1. Call their customer service line.
2. Ask if you can add a verification PIN.
3. Generate a [random PIN](https://strongphrase.net/passcode) for each account.
4. Save it in your [password manager](/essentials).
5. If you can't add a PIN, ask what other security features they offer to prevent someone from pretending to be you and accessing your account.

</HowTo>
