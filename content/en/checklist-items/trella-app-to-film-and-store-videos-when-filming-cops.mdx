The cops can get access to videos on your phone either by confiscated it and hacking into it or by getting a search warrant for your cloud photos app (if you use one).

The Trella app lets you store videos, photos, and files in a more-secure way on your phone. It adds an extra layer of encryption that makes it harder for the cops to break into. If you take the videos/photos from within the app, they will never be saved to your camera roll, which prevents them from getting uploaded to the cloud.

<HowTo title="How to use the Tella app to record video">

1. **Install** the [Tella App](https://tella-app.org/)
2. **Set up a passcode that is **<u>**different than your phone passcode**</u>**.** Use a [random passcode generator](https://strongphrase.net) here. Store the passcode on your password manager.
3. **Record from within Tella:** The most secure option is to start recording your video from within Tella instead of from the normal camera app. That way, it is never saved to your usual camera roll.




**What about cloud sync in Tella? **You may choose to set up the cloud sync options if you really need to get data off your phone as soon as possible. But be aware that law enforcement can compel tech companies to turn that information over.

</HowTo>
