---
title: Uninstall apps with sensitive data (email, docs, social media, etc) on phone and laptop
slug: uninstall
preview: "Remove anything you don't want to be easily searchable by the cops."
---

The less you have on your device, the better. 

<HowTo title="Which apps to uninstall">

Consider uninstalling the following apps (on phone and laptop) if they have any sensitive data in them:

- Email & Calendar apps (if you're using your phone's built-in apps you might need to sign out from your phone's Settings > Accounts)
- Docs & notes (Google Docs, Evernote, etc.)
- Other communication apps (Slack, WhatsApp, etc.)
- Google Maps (or at minimum [disable Location History](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/3118687?sjid=15768665358815288280-NC))
- Social Media apps


**Tip: Hide the app:** You can also move an app to a hidden folder. Instructions: [iPhone](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/lock-or-hide-or-an-app-iph00f208d05/ios) and [Android](https://support.google.com/android/answer/15341885?hl=en). This will only provide a slight advantage since most cops would know to look in this folder. Deleting is much more secure.

You may also want to **delete specific content** from apps that you leave installed:

- Notes in the built-in Notes app on iOS
- Individual text message threads
- Move sensitive photos to a [Hidden Album](https://support.apple.com/en-us/104987) (or alternately, back them up somewhere and remove them entirely from your device)




<Alert type="warning">

**Empty the trash:** If you've deleted photos, notes, emails, or documents, there is often a "trash" or "recently deleted" folder where they are stored for 30 days. Make sure you go there and manually delete them immediately.

</Alert>

</HowTo>
