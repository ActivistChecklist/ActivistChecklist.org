Anytime you connect to the internet (phone or computer), your internet provider is revealing your approximate location to every site/app you use.

We know that [law enforcement agencies are using tools that to track your location](https://www.404media.co/inside-ices-tool-to-monitor-phones-in-entire-neighborhoods/) using data gathered from apps you have and ads you see. Using a VPN with ad-blocking features enabled makes it much harder for them to track you. Also, police can get a subpoena for your internet traffic from your internet provider.

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) helps mask your location and makes you slightly harder to identify.

A VPN with ad-blocking enabled is especially important on your phone.

<Alert type="warning">

**A VPN doesn't make you fully anonymous.** For sensitive browsing requiring higher anonymity, consider [Tor Browser](https://activistchecklist.org/research) as a second browser alongside your everyday one.

</Alert>

**Downsides to using a VPN**

- You will encounter more CAPTCHAs on websites
- Some websites may block VPN access, and you'll have to disable it and remember to re-enable it later
- Some streaming services might not work


If you experience odd behavior on a website, try turning off the VPN temporarily to see if it will load. But, since it is easy to forget to turn the VPN back on, the best solution is to enable [split tunneling](https://mullvad.net/en/help/split-tunneling-with-the-mullvad-app) in Mullvad. You can configure it so a secondary browser skips the VPN. For example, if you use Brave as your main browser, you can configure Chrome or Safari to bypass the VPN and only use that browser when a website isn't working.
