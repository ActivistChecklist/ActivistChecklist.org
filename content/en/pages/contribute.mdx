**We welcome your contributions!** 

We are a small all-volunteer, open-source project and the more support we have, the more impactful this project will be at serving communities on the front lines of resisting authoritarianism in the US.

## How you can support this project

**Biggest needs**

- **Translation:** We need committed translators who are familiar with the kinds of technical concepts we discuss on this site. We want to publish a complete Spanish translation before we move onto other languages.
- **Writing new guides:** We want to expand the number of authors contributing to this project. That said, we have a high bar for what we will publish. The ideal author is someone who has many years of on-the-ground experience **and** is skilled at communicating to a non-technical audience in a very clear, concise, and empowering way. We can provide editing support.
- **Coding:** We have a growing list of [issues](https://github.com/ActivistChecklist/ActivistChecklist.org/issues) that anyone with coding skills can help us work on. (See below for more on how to contribute code.)
- **Security expert review/edits:** All of our guides have been reviewed by 2–3 experts before publishing. And we know that things can change quickly. We’d love support keeping an eye on older content to make sure it’s still fully relevant.
- **Promotion and partnerships:** Once of our biggest needs is support getting this site into the hands of the people who need it most. If you’re connected to networks of activist and organizers on the front lines, we’d love your support getting this guide to them. Ideally this would look like getting our guides linked to on many different activist toolkits and organization/group websites.




**Other needs:** 

- **Social media support:** We’d love to bring on a social media manager or two. We haven’t ventured into Instagram or platforms beyond Bluesky yet, but we know we could reach a lot more people if we did.
- **Copy editing:** Our existing guides can often benefit from a solid copy edit.
- **User testing / design feedback:** If you’re a UX expert and want to help us make this site more impactful for our audience (non-technical users), we’d love you help to do user interviews, user tests, and improving the user experience.
- **Surveillance news:** We’d love support keeping our [surveillance news](/news) section up to date.
- **Graphic design:** We occasionally have design tasks that we’d love support on.


***What about donations? *** We are not currently set up to take donations. We do incur costs for running the server and occasionally hiring people to help with things like translation. If we had more donations, we could use that money to hire expert contributors. If you’re interested in donating, [let us know](/contact) and we could work it out. (Note: these won’t be tax-deductible donations.)

## Are you interested in writing a new guide?

Here’s the overview of the process we use to accept new submissions. It’s designed to help someone not put in too much work that we might not be able to use.

- **Phase 1: Exploration.** [Reach out](/contact) and let us know what guide/article you might be interested in contributing. We’ll ask you about the kinds of experience you have on this topic and we’ll ask to see some other public-facing materials you’ve created that communicate complex concepts in easy-to-understand ways.
- **Phase 2: Outline.** We will send you a template outline to draft the introduction and main steps (for checklist guides) or main content (for other articles). We’ll give feedback on the outline to make sure it is heading in a direction that will work for us before you put the time intro drafting it.
- **Phase 3: Drafting.** We will send you a template Proton Doc you can use to write a full guide/article in a format that will work for our site (checklist items, “how to” boxes, alert boxes, etc.) You complete a first draft and send it to us for review. We will find a few other experts in this area to review it and give feedback. After a few iterations, if it looks good, we will proceed to publishing.
- **Phase 4: Publishing.** We will put it on the site and announce it through our channels. All of our guides are published anonymously at this point.




## Process for contributing code

If you are a coding and want to help with the site, you can [fork our Github repo](https://github.com/ActivistChecklist/ActivistChecklist.org).

We invite you to take a look at the [list of open issues](https://github.com/ActivistChecklist/ActivistChecklist.org/issues) and select one to work on. If the description of the issue is too brief or unclear, please ask for clarification on the issue thread.
